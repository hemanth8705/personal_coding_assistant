Oceans cover more than 70 percent of Earth's surface, acting as the planet's life-support system. These vast bodies of saltwater are not merely geographic features but complex ecosystems that regulate global climate, provide half of the world's oxygen, and house an incredible diversity of life. From sunlit surface waves to the crushing depths of the Mariana Trench, oceans are integral to the survival of all species.

The role of oceans in climate regulation is critical. They absorb vast amounts of solar radiation and carbon dioxide, mitigating global warming. Ocean currents act as a global conveyor belt, distributing heat and influencing weather patterns that sustain agriculture and ecosystems worldwide. Furthermore, phytoplankton performing photosynthesis produce much of the air we breathe.

However, these resources face unprecedented threats. Pollution from plastics and industrial runoff chokes marine life and degrades habitats. Overfishing disrupts food webs, while rising temperatures and ocean acidification threaten coral reefs and shell-forming organisms. Protecting our oceans is not just an environmental imperative but a necessity for human survival. We must prioritize sustainable practices and international cooperation to ensure these blue expanses remain healthy for generations to come.
